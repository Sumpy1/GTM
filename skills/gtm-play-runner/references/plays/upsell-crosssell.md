# Upsell & Cross-sell — 7 Plays

Expansion revenue is the most leveraged form of GTM work. Net revenue retention above 120% turns a flat-acquisition business into a growing one; below 100% nothing else can save you. The mistake teams make is running expansion the same way they run new sales. It's a different motion: the relationship exists, the trust exists, and the buyer has limited patience for re-discovery.

The hierarchy:

1. **Product usage upsell** — usage signals tell you what to expand.
2. **User expansion** — adding seats, the simplest expansion play.
3. **Web visit upsell** — they're researching another product; help them.
4. **Customer upsell** — moving them to a higher tier or plan.
5. **Cross-sell targeting** — adjacent product to the same buyer.
6. **Upsell feature-specific demo** — deep-dive on a specific capability.
7. **Product feature announcement** — new feature → expansion conversation.

---

## Product Usage Upsell

**Window:** When the customer hits a usage threshold — seats, data volume, API calls, or feature usage.
**Lead channel:** In-product notification + email + AE outreach.
**Difficulty:** Expert. **Impact:** Very High.

**Why it works:** A customer hitting a usage threshold is, by definition, getting value. They're voting with their consumption. The expansion conversation isn't a sale — it's a paperwork update on a decision they've already made.

**How to run:** Need a usage signal — typically defined as "X% of their plan limit" or "Y consecutive months above threshold." Trigger CS or AE outreach when crossed.

**First touch:**

> Subject: You're at [usage milestone] — quick path forward
>
> Saw [team / company] hit [specific usage threshold] this month. Two paths: (1) we can keep things as-is and you'll start hitting the limit on [specific date], or (2) we can move you to [next tier] now and avoid the disruption. Option 2 also unlocks [one specific value]. Want to grab 20 minutes to walk through the right structure?

**Follow-up sequence:**
- Day 5: Send a written proposal with both options compared.
- Day 10: Loop in the executive sponsor on customer side.
- Day 14: Final follow-up.

**Anti-pattern:** Threatening overage charges as the lead. It frames the conversation adversarially. Lead with value of the next tier, not punishment of the current one.

## User Expansion

**Window:** When the customer adds 10%+ headcount in the buying team or hits 80% seat utilization.
**Lead channel:** Email + CS rep.
**Difficulty:** Easy. **Impact:** Very High.

**Why it works:** The simplest expansion play. They're hiring, the team is using your product, more people need access. The conversion to expansion is near-automatic if you catch it at the right moment.

**How to run:** Watch for hiring signals (LinkedIn, news, headcount disclosures) AND seat utilization. Both are leading indicators.

**First touch:**

> Subject: New hires + seats
>
> Saw [team] is growing — congrats. Wanted to flag: you're at [N] of [M] seats, and at the rate the team is adding, you'll hit the limit in [estimate]. Want me to send a clean expansion proposal so it's not a fire drill?

**Anti-pattern:** Waiting until they hit 100% seat utilization. By then, someone's been blocked from logging in for two weeks and the expansion conversation is now an apology conversation.

## Web Visit Upsell

**Window:** 24-72 hours from the visit.
**Lead channel:** Email.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate. **Impact:** High.

**Why it works:** A customer visiting pages about *other* products on your site is researching expansion. They're already a buyer; they're considering more. The play is to show up at the moment of consideration.

**First touch:**

> Subject: Saw some interest in [product/feature]
>
> Noticed someone from your team was looking at [specific product / feature] on our site. No assumption that you were the one looking, but if [feature] is on your radar, the question that comes up at this stage is usually [Q1]. Happy to answer here or take 20 minutes — your call.

**Anti-pattern:** Same as cold site visit play — don't get specific about who or what page. "Some interest" is the right level of specificity.

## Customer Upsell

**Window:** Tied to renewal cycle or a major usage event.
**Lead channel:** Email + CS rep + AE.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate. **Impact:** High.

**Why it works:** Moving a customer from one tier to a higher one is easier when tied to a real moment — renewal, new use case, organizational change. The play is to identify the trigger and use it.

**How to run:** Don't pitch the upsell as "your usage is up." Pitch it as "your needs are evolving." The customer is the protagonist of the story; the upsell is what enables them.

**First touch:**

> Subject: New chapter — different setup
>
> Looking at how [team / company] is using us now vs. when we first set things up, the structure has clearly evolved — [specific observation]. Worth talking about whether the current plan still fits, or whether [next tier] would actually save you money long-term given how you're using us.

**Anti-pattern:** Lecturing the customer about their own usage. They know what they're doing. Position yourself as the partner thinking ahead, not the vendor selling more.

## Cross-sell Targeting

**Window:** 6+ months into the customer relationship. Before that, you're still proving out the original purchase.
**Lead channel:** Email + AE.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate. **Impact:** Medium.

**Why it works:** Adding adjacent products to an existing customer is operationally easier than landing a new account. They know you, they trust you, the procurement path is faster. The play is to identify the right buyer for the new product — often a different persona than the original.

**How to run:** Map your products to buyer personas. The cross-sell motion is essentially a new sale to a new buyer at an existing account. Multi-thread it from the start.

**Anti-pattern:** Selling the second product to the first product's buyer when they're not the right persona. They'll either say no or become a blocker. Map to the right persona first.

## Product Feature Announcement

**Window:** 7-14 days from launch, then targeted to high-fit accounts.
**Lead channel:** Email.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate. **Impact:** Medium.

**Why it works:** A new feature gives you a non-pushy reason to re-engage existing customers. The play is to filter your customer base to those who would specifically benefit, not blast everyone.

**First touch:**

> Subject: New — [feature name]
>
> We just shipped [feature]. Reaching out specifically because of [specific reason it fits their use case — e.g., "you mentioned [X] in our last review" or "you're one of the heaviest users of [related feature]"]. Worth 15 minutes to walk through what it does for your setup?

**Anti-pattern:** Mass blast to all customers. Customers can tell when an email is generic. Filter to the actual fit.

## Upsell Feature-Specific Demo

**Window:** When the customer expresses interest in a specific capability they don't yet have access to.
**Lead channel:** Calendar booking.
**Difficulty:** Expert. **Impact:** High.

**Why it works:** A specific capability demo to an existing customer converts at much higher rates than a general pitch. They know your product; they're asking about an extension.

**How to run:** This isn't a play in the "send an email" sense. It's a request for a focused 30-minute session on one specific capability. The work is making sure the demo is actually about the customer's specific situation, not the standard demo script.

**Anti-pattern:** Running the standard demo script to an existing customer. They've seen the basics. Walk them through how the new capability solves *their* specific problem with their actual data.
