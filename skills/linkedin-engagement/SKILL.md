# LinkedIn Engagement

Write LinkedIn engagement that feels specific, useful, and low-pressure. Ground every message in visible context.

## Use When

- User wants connection notes, comments, InMail, follow-ups, or LinkedIn touch sequences.
- User provides a prospect profile, company page, post, persona, account context, or campaign goal.
- User needs social selling copy before email, after intent spike, after event attendance, or after content engagement.
- User asks for persona-specific LinkedIn messaging by role, seniority, stage, or trigger.

## Engagement Pattern

Strong LinkedIn engagement has:

- Context: post, role, company initiative, event, job change, shared category, or relevant signal.
- Point of view: useful observation, not flattery.
- Soft bridge: connects context to buyer priority without pitching too early.
- Small ask: connect, compare notes, share benchmark, ask one question, or offer resource.
- Native tone: conversational, short, and not email pasted into LinkedIn.

## Evidence To Use

Prefer source-backed signals:

- Prospect profile headline, role scope, recent posts, comments, talks, podcasts, or panels.
- Company news, website, hiring, funding, product launches, customer stories.
- Shared groups, events, category conversations, partner ecosystem, or competitor movement.
- CRM notes, opportunity stage, prior touches, intent data, or known buying committee role.

## Process

1. Identify LinkedIn surface: connection note, comment, InMail, follow-up, or sequence.
2. Capture prospect context and account trigger.
3. Choose one message angle: insight, question, relevant proof, peer pattern, or useful resource.
4. Draft short copy. Default connection note under 300 characters.
5. Provide variants by tone when useful: direct, thoughtful, executive, or casual.

## Output Format

Return:

```markdown

### Recommended Message
[Copy]

### Variants

- Thoughtful: [copy]
- Executive: [copy]

| Touch | Channel | Message goal | Copy |
|-------|---------|--------------|------|
| 1 | Connection note | [goal] | [copy] |
| 2 | Comment | [goal] | [copy] |
| 3 | InMail | [goal] | [copy] |

- [Signal]: [why it matters]

- [Assumption or data gap]
```

## Guardrails

- Do not invent shared connections, post content, job changes, or personal details.
- Do not write spammy praise like "love your post" unless evidence supports it.
- Keep LinkedIn copy shorter than email.
- Avoid hard pitch in first touch unless user explicitly asks.
- If profile/post context is missing, ask for it or write a safe role-based version.
